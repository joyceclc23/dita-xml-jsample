3.2. Thermal Management in Power Calculator

Lattice Radiant software Power Calculator utilizes the ambient temperature (°C) to calculate the junction temperature (°C) based on the JA for the targeted device. You can also provide the airflow values (in Linear Feet per Minute or LFM, which is also the same as Cubic Feet per Minute) to obtain a more accurate junction temperature value. Thermal Environment options in the Power Calculators provide a thermal impedances for JEDEC (4”x 4”, 2S2P), small (6”x 6”, 6 layered board), medium (8”x8”, 8 layered board), and large (10”x10”, or larger board). There are also options to select copper heatsinks of different sized fins. These impedances and options are provided as a guidance. You have an option to provide their own thermal impedance, too. As each application board can be different, it is recommended that accurate thermal impedance be provided. These can be obtained by measuring using a thermal lab or by using thermal simulation software.
